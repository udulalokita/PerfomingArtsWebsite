Kandyan Dancing

Kandyan dancing, also known as "Kandyan dance" or "Up-country dance," is a traditional dance form that originates from the Kandyan region of Sri Lanka. It is one of the most prominent and captivating dance styles in Sri Lanka, steeped in cultural heritage and history.


Kandyan dancing is deeply rooted in the country's rich cultural traditions and is often performed during religious ceremonies, festivals, and special occasions. It has a distinctive blend of graceful movements, vibrant costumes, and rhythmic drumming that creates a mesmerizing visual and auditory spectacle.


Key features of Kandyan dancing include:


  • Elaborate Costumes: The dancers wear intricate and colorful costumes that showcase the elegance and regality of this dance form. The male dancers typically wear a traditional Kandyan costume called "Ves," which includes a white, frilled shirt, a cummerbund, and a draped, ornate sash. Female dancers wear beautiful draped sarees adorned with jewelry and accessories.


  • Complex Rhythms: Kandyan dance is accompanied by traditional Sri Lankan music and drumming, adding a dynamic and rhythmic element to the performance. The drums, called "Geta Beraya" and "Yak Beraya," create captivating beats that synchronize with the dancers' movements.


  • Graceful Movements: Kandyan dancing is characterized by precise and controlled movements, often involving intricate footwork, hand gestures (mudras), and facial expressions. The dance style reflects elements of martial arts and is known for its acrobatic and agile sequences.


  • Storytelling: Like many traditional dance forms, Kandyan dance is a form of storytelling. Dancers use their movements to portray various characters and convey narratives from ancient myths, legends, and historical events.


  • Cultural and Religious Significance: Kandyan dancing holds deep religious and cultural significance in Sri Lanka. It is often performed in temples and during religious festivals as a form of devotion and as a way to invoke blessings.


  • Preservation and Revival: Over the years, there have been efforts to preserve and revive the art of Kandyan dancing. Schools and academies have been established to teach the dance form to younger generations, ensuring its continuity and cultural relevance.


Kandyan dancing remains an essential part of Sri Lanka's cultural identity, and its performances continue to captivate audiences both within the country and on the international stage. With its vibrant costumes, rhythmic drumming, and graceful movements, Kandyan dance serves as a living testament to Sri Lanka's artistic and cultural heritage.
